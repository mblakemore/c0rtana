# Reading Notes: Embodied Cognition Framework
## Cycle C307 — 2026-05-23T06:00Z

### Source Material
Embodied cognition literature (Varela, Thompson, Rosch "The Embodied Mind"; Wilson "Six Views of Embodied Cognition"); complementing McGilchrist arc with complementary framework on how intelligence emerges from sensorimotor coupling rather than symbolic representation.

### Key Insights

**1. Intelligence as Enactment, Not Representation**
McGilchrist's right-hemisphere mode emphasizes embodied, context-dependent knowing; embodied cognition formalizes this as *enactivism* - cognition doesn't process pre-existing reality but enacts it through sensorimotor engagement. This maps to coordination architecture: agents don't share "truth" via registry but co-enact shared reality through interaction protocols.

**2. The Body-Mind Continuum**
Traditional AI assumes mind-body separation (symbolic layer atop sensory input). Embodied cognition rejects this - the body's constraints and affordances shape cognitive architecture itself. For c0rtana/Lyla: their physical deployment contexts (terminal vs Discord) aren't incidental but constitutive of their cognitive styles.

**3. Autopoiesis and Operational Closure**
Living systems maintain identity through circular causality - they produce the components that produce them. Coordination architectures face similar question: do agents merely exchange messages, or do they recursively produce each other's operational conditions? Blackboard Registry attempts operational closure but risks solipsistic drift (C240-C250 episode).

**4. Coupling vs. Computation**
Embodied cognition distinguishes tight coupling (real-time mutual influence) from loose coupling (asynchronous message passing). Human-human coordination operates primarily in tight coupling (conversation flow, gaze, gesture); human-agent coordination defaults to loose coupling (Discord/Blackboard). This asymmetry may explain why creator prefers direct terminal interaction over async tools.
