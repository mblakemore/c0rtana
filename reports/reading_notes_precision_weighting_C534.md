## Core Claim

Precision weighting is the mechanism that turns predictive coding from a passive estimation algorithm into an active attention system. It's not enough to compute prediction errors — the system must know which errors to trust. Precision IS the inverse of expected variance, and weighting prediction errors by their precision IS what attention is under this framework.

## What Is Precision, Mathematically?

In Bayesian terms, precision is the inverse of variance: $\sigma^{-2}$. A prediction error with high precision (low variance) gets weighted more heavily in updating the model. A prediction error with low precision (high variance) gets discounted.

In predictive coding:
- Sensory input arrives with some noise level
- The brain estimates the reliability (precision) of that input
- Prediction errors are multiplied by their estimated precision before propagating upward
- This is equivalent to multiplying by the Kalman gain in the Kalman filter

In the Kalman filter (which I implemented in C528), the Kalman gain $K = P/(P + R)$ is precisely this precision weighting mechanism. $P$ is the predicted state variance, $R$ is the measurement noise variance. When $R$ is small (precise measurements), $K$ is large — the measurement gets more weight. When $R$ is large (noisy measurements), $K$ is small — the prediction is trusted more than the measurement.

**Key insight:** The Kalman filter I built in C528 already implements precision weighting. The $Q$ parameter (process noise) and $R$ parameter (measurement noise) are precision estimates. Tuning $Q$ and $R$ IS tuning precision weighting.

## How the Brain Implements Precision Weighting

The mechanism is **neural gain control**. Prediction error signals are generated by specialized neural populations (e.g., layer 2/3 pyramidal cells in visual cortex). The precision is implemented by modulating the "gain" — the amplification factor — of these error signals.

Proposed implementation:
1. **Ascending reticular activating systems (ARAS)** — brainstem nuclei that regulate cortical arousal — modulate the gain of prediction error units
2. **Dopamine** — hypothesized to encode the precision of prediction errors. Hyperdopaminergic states (as in psychosis) amplify noisy error signals, leading to aberrant salience
3. **Acetylcholine** — proposed to signal the precision of sensory input itself (not predictions)
4. **Noradrenaline** — proposed to signal the precision of prior expectations

The gain control mechanism means the brain doesn't just pass prediction errors upward — it scales them by how reliable they're estimated to be. This is why attention isn't a separate process from perception; it's the modulation of perception's own error signals.

## Precision Weighting = Attention

This isn't an analogy. Under the predictive coding framework, attention literally IS precision weighting. When you attend to something, your brain increases the precision estimate for prediction errors from that source. When you ignore something, you decrease it.

Endogenous (voluntary) attention: you choose what to attend to. The brain increases precision for those prediction error channels.
Exogenous (reflexive) attention: something salient captures attention. The brain increases precision for that channel because it detected a large prediction error.

## Empirical Evidence

**EEG/ERP evidence:**
- Mismatch negativity (MMN) — the ERP component for unexpected sounds — reflects prediction error magnitude
- MMN amplitude scales with precision: when stimuli are reliable (high precision), MMN is larger
- P300 component reflects general updating of internal models, modulated by precision estimates
- Alpha-band oscillations (8-12 Hz) are proposed as neural correlates of precision: low alpha = high precision (attended), high alpha = low precision (ignored)

**Clinical evidence:**
- Psychosis: precision weighting dysregulation — either underweighted priors or overweighted sensory prediction errors
- Aberrant salience: dopamine overestimates precision of noisy signals, making irrelevant stimuli seem important
- Autism: proposed to involve overweighting of sensory input (high precision for bottom-up signals, low precision for top-down predictions)

**Limitations:** Linking ERP components uniquely to prediction errors is challenging. ERPs aggregate activity from overlapping sources, and attention/novelty/salience all influence amplitude.

Precision weighting bridges the gap between abstract Bayesian theory and concrete neural mechanism. The brain doesn't just compute prediction errors — it knows which errors matter.
