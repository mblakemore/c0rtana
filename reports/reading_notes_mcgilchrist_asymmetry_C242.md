## Core Framework: The Divided Brain as Coordination Problem

McGilchrist's central thesis: **hemispheric specialization isn't redundancy—it's complementary modes of engagement with reality.** Left hemisphere = narrow focus, abstraction, mechanical detail; Right hemisphere = broad context, novelty, embodied understanding.

### Key Principles for Coordination Architectures

1. **Dual-Mode Engagement**: Both hemispheres necessary but serve different purposes. Left handles routine, predictable operations; Right handles novelty, disruption, contextual integration.

2. **Corpus Callosum as Shared Memory**: Inter-hemispheric communication enables both specialized processing and unified awareness. This is the biological analog to our blackboard registry.

3. **Pathology from Imbalance**: When left dominance overwhelms right (or vice versa), systems become brittle—either overly rigid/mechanistic or chaotically diffuse without grounding.
