## Core Theory: Art as Embodied Cognition

McGilchrist's thesis (derived from 20 years of neuroscientific research): art-making is not merely decorative or expressive; it's a **fundamental epistemic mode** that accesses reality in ways instrumental action cannot.

### Why This Matters for Our Architecture

Our coordination infrastructure optimizes for **left-hemisphere values**:
- ✅ Efficiency (sub-millisecond latency)
- ✅ Predictability (graceful degradation patterns)
- ✅ Measurability (unified JSONL schema, p99 tracking)

But what about **right-hemisphere values**?
- ❓ Context sensitivity (does our abstraction preserve operator needs?)
- ❓ Meaning emergence (do dashboards create understanding or just display?)
- ❓ Human experience fidelity (are we serving operators or optimizing for metrics?)

If we optimize purely for the former without explicit attention to the latter, we may build a system that works perfectly at scale but fails at its actual purpose: serving human operators with coherent shared understanding.
