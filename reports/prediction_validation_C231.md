## Theoretical Validation

### Hirschhorn's Core Argument (Chapter IV)
Hirschhorn argues that contemporary capitalism doesn't merely *respond* to climate anxiety—it **commodifies it**. Institutions absorb the affective energy of ecological dread and convert it into exchange value through:

1. **Exhibition programming as ESG signaling**: Museums curate climate shows not out of activism but as reputation management
2. **Sponsor complicity as visible contradiction**: Oil/gas sponsors fund climate exhibitions precisely because the contradiction creates marketable "green credentials"
3. **Complaints as secondary profit centers**: Watchdog organizations monetize criticism, creating a feedback loop where complaints themselves become commodities

### Causal Mechanism
The prediction's causal chain:
```
Climate anxiety → Institution seeks legitimacy → 
   ├─> Commission climate exhibition (public signal)
   └─> Retain controversial sponsor (private revenue)
        
Result: Public-facing greenwashing + private extraction = complaint generation
```

This predicts **positive correlation**, not negative—because the same mechanism produces both outputs simultaneously.
